KAWASAKI NINJA H2

CONCEPT NINJA H2

"The Ninja H2" was created as a closed-course model with the goal of being the best motorbike possible, allowing for a pure pursuit of performance without the constraints that come with street riding. Strong acceleration, an extremely high top speed, super sport-level handling capability, and a sensory experience that is unmatched by anything riders can find today are all provided by its more than 300 PS supercharged engine.

The concept of the Kawasaki Ninja H2 is to create an ultra-high-performance motorcycle that combines groundbreaking technology, immense power, and innovative design to push the boundaries of speed and handling. Powered by a supercharged 998cc inline-4 engine, the Ninja H2 produces over 200 horsepower, making it one of the most powerful production motorcycles. Its aerodynamic design incorporates advanced features like a ram-air intake system to boost engine performance and enhance stability at high speeds. Additionally, the bike is equipped with cutting-edge technologies such as electronic throttle control, traction control, and advanced ride modes to ensure a safe and thrilling riding experience.

The track-only version, the Ninja H2R, takes the concept even further, with over 300 horsepower and lightweight carbon fiber components. Overall, the Ninja H2 is designed to deliver exceptional acceleration, top speed, and handling, making it a true marvel of modern motorcycle engineering.

AERODYNAMICS SPEED

Wind resistance rises dramatically with increasing speed. High power and slick aerodynamics were required in order to function in the ultra-high speed range. After the supercharged engine met the power needs, the next phase was to create bodywork that would reduce drag and increase control at extremely high speeds.
